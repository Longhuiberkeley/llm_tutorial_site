Chapter 1: Intuition — How LLMs "Think"

Building intuitive mental models of how LLMs work by revealing that LLMs are just "autocomplete × 999" — not magic, not intelligence, just prediction.

📱

🤖

Large Language Model

Roses are...

Predicts entire distributions of knowledge across contexts.

LLM = autocomplete × 999
📝

TL;DR (Summary)

While your phone predicts one word at a time based on narrow patterns, an LLM predicts entire sequences by understanding the statistical relationships between millions of concepts simultaneously.

⚠️

Common Error

Thinking LLMs "think" or "know" facts. They are actually performing high-dimensional statistical completion. They don't have a database of truths; they have a map of probabilities.

🔍 1.2 Word Distance — Which Words Are "Close"?

Words have "distance" in meaning-space. This is the core intuition of how LLMs process information.

📝

TL;DR

Words have "distance" between them based on meaning. Similar words like "lion" and "tiger" are close. Unrelated words like "lion" and "banana" are far.

Meaning-Space

Think of it as a giant map where every word has a specific coordinate. Words with similar meanings are located near each other.
