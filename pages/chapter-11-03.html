11.3 Is Your Company Ready? — The Big Data Lesson

History repeats itself. The same mistakes made during the big data era are being made again with AI — often by the same companies.

🎯 Core Goals

  • Draw clear parallels between the big data era and the current AI era.
  • Help organizations assess their readiness before investing heavily in AI.
Before the AI rush, there was the big data rush. Companies built data lakes that became data swamps. The lesson: technology without foundation is expensive failure. AI is repeating the same pattern.

History Rhymes

The year is 2015. "Data is the new oil." Every company needs a data strategy, a Chief Data Officer, a data lake. The consultants are booked months out.

A few years later: many of those data lakes were abandoned. Data was siloed across departments. Quality was poor. Nobody agreed on definitions ("Is 'active customer' someone who bought once or twice this year?"). The dashboards nobody used. The insights nobody trusted.

The companies that succeeded weren't necessarily the ones who moved fastest. They were the ones who built foundations first: data governance, quality standards, clear ownership, agreed definitions.

The Parallel Is Exact

The AI rush of 2024–2026 is following the same pattern:

Big Data Era AI Era
"Data is the new oil" "AI will transform every business"
Data lakes built without governance AI tools deployed without process clarity
Poor data quality → bad insights Poor process documentation → bad automation
"We have the data, why don't insights work?" "We have the AI, why doesn't it work?"

The root cause is the same: rushing to the technology before the foundations are ready.

The AI Readiness Checklist

Before investing heavily in AI, ask honestly:

Process documentation:

  • Can you describe your key workflows in enough detail for an outsider to follow them?
  • Are the exceptions and edge cases written down, or only in employees' heads?

Data quality:

  • Is the data your AI will use clean, consistent, and accessible?
  • Do different teams use the same terms for the same things?

Success metrics:

  • How will you know if the AI is working?
  • What does "good" look like for the task you're automating?

Team skills:

  • Do you have people who can evaluate AI outputs critically?
  • Is there someone who can translate between business needs and technical implementation?

If you can't answer these, the AI project will struggle — not because AI doesn't work, but because the foundation isn't ready.

The Silver Lining

Here's what's different this time: AI-assisted development makes building the foundations dramatically faster and cheaper than it was during the big data era.

Documenting processes, cleaning data, writing specifications — AI can help with all of these. The tools make it faster to get ready for the tools. You just need to know you need to get ready first.

One of the best AI use cases during readiness preparation: have an AI interview your key employees about their workflows and draft the documentation. What used to take a business analyst weeks now takes days. Use AI to accelerate your AI readiness.

📝 Key Concepts

  • History repeats: The big data era's failures are the roadmap for AI adoption failures
  • Foundation first: Process clarity, data quality, and success metrics before technology
  • The same companies: Many organizations making AI mistakes today made identical big data mistakes
  • AI accelerates readiness: Ironically, AI can help you prepare for AI — document processes, clean data, write specs
  • Strategic timing beats speed: The companies that win aren't first — they're careful
