3.2 The Sandwich — How Messages Really Stack Up

See the full payload structure that gets sent to the LLM on every turn.

🎯 Core Goals

  • Show the full "payload" structure sent to the LLM.
  • Understand that the "reply" is just the bottom layer of a huge sandwich.
Every time you send a message, the AI doesn't just see your words. It sees a giant "sandwich" containing hidden instructions, your past messages, and even documents you've uploaded.

👁️ Visuals & Interactives

What's Actually Sent to the AI?

Toggle between the User View and the Full Sandwich

How are you?
I am fine, thank you. How about you?
I'm great. I need help writing an essay on American Culture in the 1990s.
🔒 System Prompt (Hidden)
You are a helpful, professional writing assistant. Maintain an academic tone. Use US English.
📚 Context Injection (Files/Docs)
[User preference: Prefers MLA style. Previous essay data: "90s fashion" uploaded.]
👤 User Message #1
How are you?
🤖 LLM Reply #1
I am fine, thank you. How about you?
👤 User Message #2
I'm great. I need help writing an essay on American Culture in the 1990s.
🧠 Internal Thinking Tokens (Hidden)
The user is asking about 90s culture. I should combine the MLA preference with 90s fashion data...
✨ Generating Final Reply...
Certainly! To write an essay on American culture in the 1990s, we should explore...

Every Turn #2, the FULL STACK above is sent to the LLM at once.

📝 Key Concepts

  • Layered Payload: Your message is wrapped in several layers:
    • System Prompt: Hidden instructions like "Be concise."
    • Pre-prompt / Context: Injected data like RAG results or files.
    • Conversation History: All previous messages from both you and the AI.
    • Thinking Tokens: (For reasoning models) The model's internal work.
  • Stateless Re-reading: Every turn, the LLM processes ALL of this at once to generate its next reply.
  • The Magic Trick: What you see as a simple chat is actually a complex stack being replayed every single time you hit enter.
