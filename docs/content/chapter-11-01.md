## 🎯 Core Goals
- Shift the framing from "AI replaces jobs" to "AI supercharges people."
- Show concrete examples of augmentation in practice.

:::callout-tldr
The most successful AI deployments don't eliminate jobs — they amplify what humans already do well. A lawyer with AI can review 10x more cases. A designer with AI ships 20 mockups before lunch. The human is still essential.
:::

## Two Narratives, Very Different Outcomes

**The Replacement Narrative:** "AI will automate X% of all jobs by 20XX." Workers grow anxious. People hide their AI use to avoid appearing replaceable. Teams resist adoption. Projects fail from the inside.

**The Augmentation Narrative:** "AI lets our team do more with what they're already great at." People experiment openly. Skills evolve. Adoption accelerates. Teams find real wins.

The second narrative isn't just more comforting — it's more accurate for how most AI deployments actually work.

## Three Real Augmentation Scenarios

**The Lawyer + AI**
Sarah reviews 3 new cases per week. With AI helping draft analysis, cross-reference precedents, and spot inconsistencies, she reviews 30. She's still the lawyer — making the strategic calls, building the client relationship, signing off on every document. The AI handles the mechanical volume.

**The Designer + AI**
A product designer used to spend two days producing 3–5 concept mockups. Now they generate 20 variations in a morning using AI image tools, then spend their afternoon on the ones that actually show promise — applying the judgment and taste that the AI lacks. Creative output increases; tedious iteration decreases.

**The Support Agent + AI**
A customer support agent handles 40 tickets per day. With AI drafting initial responses and surfacing relevant knowledge base articles, they handle 120 — while spending more time on the complex cases that actually need human empathy and judgment.

In each case, the human's role shifts: *less mechanical execution, more judgment, oversight, and relationship.*

## Jobs Don't Disappear — They Change Shape

History bears this out. ATMs didn't eliminate bank tellers — the number of bank branches increased because each branch required fewer tellers, making it cheaper to open more branches. Teller work changed (less counting cash, more relationship banking), but teller employment didn't collapse.

The same pattern has appeared in every major technology shift. AI will be no different: roles evolve, the composition of work changes, and the humans who adapt well end up more valuable — not less.

:::callout-error
"AI will automate my team's jobs" is a self-fulfilling prophecy if leaders act on it without nuance. Most tasks involve judgment, relationships, context, and accountability — things LLMs genuinely can't provide. Focus on where humans add irreplaceable value, then let AI handle the volume.
:::

## 📝 Key Concepts

- **Augmentation vs. replacement:** The framing matters — it shapes how people adopt AI
- **Mechanical execution → judgment:** AI takes the volume; humans provide the oversight
- **Historical pattern:** Technology shifts change job composition, they rarely eliminate entire categories
- **Leaders set the tone:** If leadership models augmentation thinking, teams follow
